About HackTrin

HackTrin VIII is the eighth iteration of Trinity School’s annual hackathon. Middle and high schoolers, of any experience level, can participate. You can form teams of 1-4 students, even across different schools, and work to make projects relating to coding. We'll have workshops and mentors to help you with your projects and learn! We will announce the secret theme two weeks before HackTrin, after which you can start planning (but not coding or building). Coding and building starts March 7th. You’ll present your projects to our panels of judges to win prizes in multiple categories (including a beginner category)!

*Schools have a limit of bringing three teams.

FAQ


What is a hackathon?

A hackathon is an event where teams get together and build a project relating to coding, using software or hardware components, in a short period of time.

What types of projects are allowed at HackTrin VII?

Anything you want, as long as it involves some coding component and relates to the theme. Popular projects in the past have included websites, apps, games, gagdets, or whatever else you come up with!

Is there a theme at HackTrin VII?

We're announcing the theme for HackTrin VII on the website two weeks before, as well as notifying all participants by email.

Who can come to HackTrin VII?

HackTrin VII is open to any current high school or middle school student.

How will HackTrin VII run during COVID?

HackTrin VII will run this year on Discord! All individuals must have a Discord account and the link to the server will be sent out prior to the event.
