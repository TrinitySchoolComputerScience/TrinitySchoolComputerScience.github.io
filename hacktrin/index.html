About HackTrin

HackTrin IX is the ninth iteration of Trinity School’s annual hackathon. Middle and high schoolers, of any experience level, can participate. You can form teams of 1-4 students, even across different schools, and work to make projects relating to coding. We'll have workshops and mentors to help you with your projects and learn! We will announce the secret theme two weeks before HackTrin, after which you can start planning (but not coding or building). Coding and building starts February 27th. You’ll present your projects to our panels of judges to win prizes in multiple categories (including a beginner category)!

FAQ


What is a hackathon?

A hackathon is an event where teams get together and build a project relating to coding, using software or hardware components, in a short period of time.

What types of projects are allowed at HackTrin?

Anything you want, as long as it involves some coding component and relates to the theme. Popular projects in the past have included websites, apps, games, gagdets, or whatever else you come up with!

Is there a theme at HackTrin?

We're announcing the theme for HackTrin on the website one week before, as well as notifying all participants by email. Once you receive the theme, you can brainstorm ideas, but all work and coding must be done during the competition window.

Who can come to HackTrin?

HackTrin is open to any current high school or middle school student.

How will HackTrin run during COVID?

HackTrin will run this year with a hybrid model. Fully vaccinated participants are encouraged to come in person at Trinity school! We will also be offering a virtual option on Discord. All virtual individuals must have a Discord account and the link to the server will be sent out prior to the event.

What is Discord?

Discord is the platform that virtual HackTrin will be run through this year. Discord is a voice/video/text platform and easily facilitates team events. To create a Discord account click here.

What do I need to bring?

Please bring your own computer and charger. Charging outlets will be accessible during the competition.

Awards


Best Middle School -- The middle school project that stands out overall


Best Design -- The project that has the best construction and implementation


Best Overall -- The project that excels in every category


Best Use of the Theme -- The project that creatively and effectively uses the theme


Judges' Choice -- The project that the judges think is especially inventive, holds a lot of promise, or stands out in ways different from the other categories
